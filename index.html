About

I'm a Software Developer and IT Operations specialist based in New York City. During my work as a DevOps Engineer and as IT Support, I've had unique opportunities in maintaining on-premises and cloud infrastucture. On different occasions, I have been trusted to lead cross-functional teams— of both technical and non-technical backgrounds— in completing projects that were pivotal to those respective teams. In those experiences and in the sum of my journey with technology, I have acquired and gained confidence at skills in web Application Development, automation, network infrastructure and security, cloud services, reliability, and business communications.

On my personal time— I'm a martial arts teacher, I like to work on cars, and I like to make music. My favorite styles include R&B, jazz, and pop- especially in an 80s style. Since I like to record, I'm also experienced with Audio/Video equipment and editing software (FL Studio, Adobe Premiere).

Other interests of mine include Machine Learning and Information Security. Most of my experience has been with training and inferencing pre-trained models, including LLMs. One thing I think about is how packages like TensorFlow, Scikit-Learn, and PyTorch utilize memory on the host's CPU or GPU, and that not much is widely known about how these packages utilize memory. This makes me wonder what potential vulnerabilities lie with these packages.

Offhand, my go-to stack includes Python/NodeJS, React, and MySQL/MongDB (based on need). To deploy services more efficiently, I love to use Docker containers.
